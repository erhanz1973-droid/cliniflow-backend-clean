3-second scan test (coordinator ergonomics)

Pick 10 random leads. For each, a coordinator should answer in ~3 seconds without opening detail:

  • Who needs attention? (name + urgent flag if any)
  • What is blocking progression? (one short line)
  • Who are we waiting on? (patient vs clinic)
  • What should happen next? (next action line)
  • Inbox does not feel like a CRM dashboard (badge overload, metric noise)

Workflow stress test (20–30 leads)

Run on staging before production. Goal: friction and confusion, not feature coverage.

  • 20–30 simultaneous leads — scroll and queue switch stay fast
  • Incomplete uploads (photos / X-ray) — correct queue + “waiting on patient”
  • Implant-tagged leads — implant queue; blocker text is plain language
  • Cosmetic-tagged leads — cosmetic queue
  • Patients who abandon midway — inactive / stale timing visible
  • Pending doctor review — clinic wait + clear next step
  • Multilingual conversations — language visible in detail, not noisy in list
  • Coordinator handoff (Human Active) — human follow-up queue + “Send coordinator reply”
  • Consultation-ready leads — secondary queue; not mixed with blockers

Patient Treatment Guide (friction)

  • Checklist feels short (not hospital CRM)
  • Full journey hidden until expanded
  • No tourism / package sales language in patient flow
  • Upload prompts calm; no upload fatigue on first visit
